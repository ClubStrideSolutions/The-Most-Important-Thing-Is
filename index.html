Personal Career Narrative 20 min

Craft a compelling career narrative that motivates you:

  • Transform your passions into a meaningful career story
  • Identify your unique strengths and how they align with your goals
  • Create a personal narrative that keeps you focused when challenges hit
+

Key Concepts

Career Narrative: Your unique story that connects your personal strengths to your professional goals

Inspiration Mining: Finding motivation in the journeys of successful professionals in your field

Resilience Anchors: Powerful reminders of your "why" that help you push through obstacles

Activity: Crafting Your Personal Career Story

Materials: Paper and pen or digital notes

Instructions:

  1. Take 5 minutes for quiet reflection on what excites you most about your future
  2. Complete these prompts to craft your personal narrative:
    • Vision: "I'm fired up about ___ in my future career, and staying focused helps me by ___."
    • Reality Check: "I know it's challenging to ___, especially when ___."
    • Evidence: "I've observed that people who succeed in this field typically ___."
    • Inspiration: "A success story that resonates with me is ___ because ___."
    • Declaration: "That's why I'm committed to ___ no matter what obstacles arise."
  3. Review and refine your narrative until it genuinely inspires you
  4. Optional: Volunteers may share insights during open discussion

Strategic Career Thinking 15 min

Master the skill of strategic decision-making:

  • Learn how top performers think differently about daily choices
  • Connect your current classes and activities to long-term career success
  • Identify and eliminate habits that sabotage your potential
+

Key Concepts

Strategic Thinking: Looking beyond immediate situations to evaluate how today's decisions impact tomorrow's opportunities

Purpose-Driven Decisions: Making choices based on your long-term vision rather than short-term convenience

Career Compounding: How small daily habits and choices accumulate to create major career advantages or setbacks

Examples of Strategic Career Thinking

  • Basic thinking: "I'll just take the easiest electives to boost my GPA."
  • Strategic thinking: "Which electives will give me skills that stand out on my resume and actually help in my target career?"
  • Basic thinking: "I'll go out tonight and worry about the project tomorrow."
  • Strategic thinking: "If I finish this project tonight, I can use tomorrow for networking at the career fair instead."

Career Vision Board 20 min

Visualize your path to success:

  • Create a visual roadmap of your career goals and milestones
  • Identify potential roadblocks and strategies to overcome them
  • Design a visual reminder that keeps you motivated daily
+

Activity: Creating Your Personal Career Vision Board

Materials: Paper, colored pens, markers

Instructions:

  1. Take a few minutes for quiet reflection about your ultimate career aspirations
  2. Draw or write your ultimate career goal in the center of your paper
  3. Add images and words that represent key milestones in education, internships, skills, etc.
  4. Include specific habits that support your success (focus time, healthy choices, avoiding distractions)
  5. Add your "strategic thinking" reminders around the edges
  6. This is for your personal use—consider creating a digital version later as your phone wallpaper

Remember: This is a personal document that represents your unique vision—there's no right or wrong way to create it.

Your Personal 7-Day Challenge

Choose 2–3 of these actions that feel most valuable for your journey:

  1. Each morning, spend 2 minutes reflecting on your career vision
  2. Before important decisions, ask: "Does this move me toward or away from my goals?"
  3. Identify one time-wasting habit and replace it with a career-building activity
  4. Research a role model in your field and note what you can learn from their path
  5. Experiment with a productivity technique like Pomodoro or time blocking
  6. Create a personal tracking system for your progress (journal, app, etc.)

This is your personal journey—customize these suggestions to fit your unique style and goals.
