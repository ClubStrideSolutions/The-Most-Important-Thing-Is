Workshop Activities

Click on any activity to see details and instructions. We’ll use small notebooks and pens for brief reflections or note-taking.

Personal Career Reflection 10 min

Start with individual self-discovery:

  • Explore what truly motivates you about the future
  • Consider possible career paths or next steps
  • Set a personal intention for this workshop
+

Activity: Quick Journaling

Using your small notebook and pen, jot down answers to these prompts:

  • Motivation: “What topics or tasks energize me the most?”
  • Future Dream: “Five years from now, I’d love to be _____.”
  • Barriers: “One challenge I need to address is _____.”

No need to share unless you choose to during our open discussions!

Personal Career Narrative 20 min

Shape a clear, inspiring career story that fits your unique goals:

  • Translate your experiences into a compelling vision
  • Highlight leadership moments and skill-building experiences
  • Stay focused when obstacles arise
+

Key Concepts

Career Narrative: Your story connecting personal strengths and professional aims

Inspiration Sources: Role models or past successes that fuel your determination

Resilience Reminders: Phrases or memories that keep you going when things get tough

Activity: Drafting Your Story

Instructions:

  1. Take a couple of minutes to think about what energizes you about your future career or leadership potential.
  2. Use these prompts to piece together your narrative:
    • Vision: “My goal is to _____ because _____.”
    • Challenge: “A hurdle I face is _____, but I can overcome it by _____.”
    • Role Models: “Someone I admire for their leadership is _____ because _____.”
    • Commitment: “This matters to me because _____.”
  3. Review your narrative and adjust until it truly resonates with you.

Volunteers are welcome to share after drafting.

Strategic Career Thinking 15 min

Make daily choices that align with your career goals:

  • Understand how small actions add up in the long run
  • Spot and replace habits that undermine your progress
  • Stay goal-focused, even when life feels hectic
+

Key Concepts

Strategic Thinking: Linking your day-to-day decisions to long-term goals

Purpose-Driven Choices: Evaluating options based on your career vision

Career Compounding: How minor daily habits can create major advantages—or setbacks—over time

Real-Life Examples

  • Routine vs. Growth: Attending optional training could open new job prospects.
  • Connections Count: Showing initiative at a community project can lead to better references or mentorships.
  • Study & Skills: Mastering financial literacy now could lead to greater earnings and savings later.

Career Vision Board 20 min

Visualize your growth and career milestones:

  • Sketch or write out key goals in your notebook
  • Mark possible roadblocks and ways around them
  • Create a visual reminder to review daily
+

Activity: Mapping Your Milestones

Instructions:

  1. In the center of a journal page, write or draw your top career or leadership goal.
  2. Add branching lines for key steps (e.g., skill certifications, volunteer experiences, or finishing school).
  3. Note any habits or learning resources you’ll need to succeed (like setting aside study time or budgeting).
  4. Keep your vision board for daily motivation.

Feel free to share ideas or get feedback from peers.

Hands-On Leadership Challenge 15 min

Practice real-time leadership in a fun group scenario:

  • Work in small teams to solve a quick challenge
  • Observe how leadership and teamwork come into play
  • Discuss insights about communication and trust
+

Activity: Leadership in Action

Scenario Example: Your team needs to organize a local event with limited time and resources. How do you assign roles, motivate the group, and address obstacles quickly?

  1. Form small groups and select a leader/facilitator.
  2. Brainstorm a plan, keeping in mind limited resources and realistic timelines.
  3. Share your group’s approach briefly with others.

Notice how decisions are made, tasks are assigned, and feedback is given—these are key lessons you can apply in any future role.

Open Forum: Career Insights 10 min

Share perspectives and advice on next steps:

  • Discuss experiences in work, community, or personal growth
  • Ask supervisors and peers about transition tips
  • Offer or request guidance on job searching or skill-building
+

Discussion Prompts

  • What recent challenge helped you grow the most?
  • Any proven strategies for staying organized (study schedules, finances, etc.)?
  • How can we use teamwork to achieve shared goals?

Feel free to chime in or just listen. This is a judgment-free space.

Quick Feedback 5 min

  • Suggest new topics or formats you’d like to see
  • Reflect on how you feel about your career path now
+

Activity: Workshop Feedback

Take a moment to fill out our brief feedback form (digital or paper) to let us know:

  • What inspired you today

  • Suggestions for future career/leadership topics

We appreciate your input—it helps us shape programs that fit your needs.

Personal Action Plan 10 min

Turn your insights into real-world steps:

  • Commit to a few short-term goals you can start this week
  • Note any resources (mentors, training, financial tips) you’ll need
  • Create a simple accountability method that keeps you on track
+

Key Reminders

  • Your purpose acts as a compass, guiding big and small decisions
  • Leadership grows through consistent practice—start small and build up
  • Daily habits (like setting a budget or showing up on time) compound into major career advantages
  • Open communication and seeking help early can solve problems before they escalate

Your Personal 7-Day Challenge

Pick 2–3 small steps that fit your goals:

  1. Each morning, take a minute to remind yourself why your goals matter.
  2. Check if your daily choices align with your bigger vision.
  3. Identify one unproductive habit and replace it with a skill-builder (e.g., reading about career fields of interest).
  4. Book a quick chat with a mentor or staff member for personalized guidance.
  5. Experiment with time-blocking to stay on top of tasks or studies.
  6. Review or start a simple budget plan to track earnings and expenses.

These small changes can spark big progress in your leadership and career journey.
